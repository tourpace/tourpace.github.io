**The Raccoon River Valley Bike Trail (RRVT) is one gem of a paved rail-to-trail in Central Iowa.** After a visit to family in NE Iowa we ventured over to Minburn, Iowa to ride portions of the RRVT. We chose Minburn because it was on the RRVT and found [Kim's Kottage](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/20814277), a nice AirBNB rental catering to cyclists.

[Raccoon River Valley Trail](https://raccoonrivervalleytrail.org/) offers 89 miles of pavement and it is possible to ride a continuous 71 mile loop through 13 different towns. Two additional towns are located on a northern out and back extension of the trail up to Jefferson, Iowa. Each town offers a trail head with parking and many of the trail heads have restrooms and water. Trail passes are required to use the trail - daily ($2/person) and annual ($10/person).

Our first ride was from Minburn to Dallas Center, Waukee, Ortonville, Adel, and almost Redfield and then back for 51 miles. We used Adel as our rest stop for snacks at a local convenience store. We saw several restaurants along the way that we would visit later for lunch, dinner, and dessert.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/HU9vGmVO9o2Chd8dthKW67oRze5QG52iBvbqAowQh3Bm7ffH2uK1KY8w_Ac1wcLh655UvFDaS_KEctSEOKyPGM8YZIJKREFUdSKBkn9UkPJQz67Co0-KHaElCfnJhO4CfLU-3VrfVCA=w2400" width="100%">

For our second ride we drove 12 miles over to Woodward, Iowa to ride the High Trestle Trail. The [High Trestle Trail](https://www.polkcountyiowa.gov/conservation/parks-trails/high-trestle-trail/) is a 25 mile paved rail-to-trail from Woodward to Ankeny. There are no trail fees but donations are always welcome.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/2dk_F7O1iEdd3QCrEPbXOhcjGnf2SodwNpd8nR6iW_Jn4hH7zYobCqiH2vZg9K5NhzvsC8Rehsv4xbprf2VSkwzIZjiLUhxQxsNKuI5xa2ZITus7HDblPbvHI1JbUlxUaqqojf7MXFg=w2400" width="100%">

The highlight is a half-mile, 13 story tall bridge that spans the Des Moines River. It was a very windy day so we rode from Woodward towards Ankeny to a restroom facility half-way between Sheldahl and Ankeny and then back for 37 miles total.

The third ride we did was and out and back of 26 miles from Minburn to Perry and Dawson. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEW7e1v2rb-aMGpxgjqBUnxjMY3IYl4ztBvz4jxDNOQHfsH3NX78q-l--NYLnZIowkh-w6v7d0TnumzJkxoGFcMEHyXOaZw_ugphSfst629m7ft0cV0Q=w2400" width="100%">

We sampled a variety of restaurants, breweries, and one winery. Lunch stops along the RRVT included [Brickyard Burgers and Brews](https://adelbrickyard.com/) in Adel, [The Handlebar](http://thehandlebardc.com/) in Dallas Center, and the Nineteen14 in Minburn. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEWM6sHWPQ1oUWC6VHhWsK1xr2IjSd3R_jbt-udloXxo08Ri2EyWr5m2ReUYLMIjiFYKXAF75Ntp5IS5PtAYrorRjzDGKIOj9wIq-F-jcbdYfPDmENEP=w2400" width="100%">

Local breweries we visited included [Kinship Brewing](https://kinshipbeer.com/) in Waukee, [Barn Town Brewing](https://www.barntownbrewing.com/) in West Des Moines, and [Exile Brewing](https://exilebrewing.com/) in Des Moines.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEW1-irLt5hV_IQBcl82G_xXGiU3Qkcq-jZ3coAyrTG86RBOfckgvFn_DjPzaOB47LuxaaCyQn9Y0n98d1Ojb39Wuc_3hr8WoytMg_lbUg2z3nliw4xC=w2400" width="100%">

Korner Kone in Dallas Center was our go-to for ice cream treats! Korner Kone is also located right on the RRVT.

After our stay in Minburn we drove over to Ankeny, Iowa to stay a few nights with college friends and to ride the [Bacoon Ride](https://bacoonride.com/). The Bacoon Ride offers a 71 mile loop ride of the Raccoon River Valley Trail with rest stops along the way that offer unique bacon snacks at each stop. Unfortunately I had started coming down with a terrible summer cold and we had to bail (I didn't want to spread my cold). So maybe another time we can tackle the Baccoon Ride.

If you ever get to the Midwest - check out the numerous bike trails in Iowa. The [Iowa Bicycle Coalition](https://www.iowabicyclecoalition.org/) or the [Iowa DOT interactive map](https://iowadot.gov/iowabikes/bikemap/home.aspx) are a great place to start for planning.
