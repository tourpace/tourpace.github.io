This was our first time to the Southwest Missouri Tandem Rally in Springfield, Missouri. Hosted by John and Jean White and the Tandem of the Ozarks Club - this is a really nice three day event (Friday - Sunday) based from the Doubletree Hotel in Springfield. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEXE68aIVx5fMcIvUTYZQLI2HjYcQdnw41qzXSK7_-VI0cyejnsLY5w1o9PVhJS-OJtPxO87CtF14UwvOePv7qlMzbWt0qj_GuPC4ypezKA2OB3cYnt7j9v-ULFW-uYSBq1VeaI3yqkhyJfhf1-QqaC1iw" width="100%">

We arrived on late Thursday afternonn and met up with friends from the Dallas/Ft. Worth area to have dinner at a really tasty Peruvian Restaurant called [Cafe Cusco](https://www.cafecusco.com/). It is located in the old Commercial Street area in Springfield. Before having dinner we decided to check-out [White River Brewing Company](https://www.whiteriverbrewing.com/) - only a block down the street from Cafe Cusco and very tasty.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEXM9EsORYoWebw-pvSZmPKynTlB1K2PZ2lmmzGcZk-qU6l5YSNKFnRPpPQJqtCwAhAI_h2YGx4du3HCO8OJvXi0R3jxc41OZCGAw8j8RwjRGrEfRhzo2nxczvLcBTjVK4Ax0z1rDYMrZFxC636iTwSwQw" width="100%">

**Friday's Ride - 33 miles (middle distance of three routes offered)**  A nice group start from the Doubletree Hotel parking lot and we ventured northeast for a nice route around Fellows Lake. Our rest stop was at the very quiant Tyler Ridge Winery where light snacks, water, and of course wine was available to taste or purchase. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEWOIlOXp6nUnMbi_QZ1sbH_EbIZHeh_GC1X2Vsz43cDiuXdcIKcXs26lk2l1rmTCNbuhIiq4BsR7UgbWDp7-EUtIv1yClwFTYU4wfJ9Kc8rNGOPF3Dtgj6Fqc40yD6PDLPcZ9EGVNi5wpdsXl4ZWUH_JA" width="100%">


We had a small mechanical issue leaving Springfield, so we rode pretty much by ourselves all the way to the winery. On our way back, we were able to ride with a small group of six tandems most of the way - we struggled to keep up with the group.

Friday evening we ventured back to Commercial Steet to try the [Pizza House](http://www.springfieldpizzahouse.com/) for thin-crust pizza! The pizza here was very good and they cut up the pies in small bite-sized squares.

After our pizza - we browsed some of the local shops and found a shopkeeper that knew the owner of the closed Pedalers Bicycle Museum and she called him to come open it up to us for a tour.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEVvIXdcwVUnmjC9EKbCMNfRkUbrIwNKss_m9deDEgCO_CgPIJEYacWJByiseSYDp6x9S40mEKCZATn2PiOyumNIAMlD-vgAFpUAvagyQZDn_S2OESwc2cQ9XQTwy6UJMTL-DQuNCnGSR2Swi0sUedPTjA" width="100%">

We were met by James Allen and he proceeded to give us a very extensive tour of the museum. If you like bicycles and collectibles of bicycling - then this is the place for you!

**Saturday's Ride - 45 Miles (middle distance of three routes offered)** Another mass start at the hotel with a route that would lead us eventually to the southeast of Springfield. Our first rest stop was at Turners, Missouri and the [Turners Station Mechantile](https://www.ozarksalive.com/stories/turners-station-mercantile-folks-keep-alive). Just a small scenic spot along the railroad that offers a little bit of everything (groceries, drinks, snacks, arts, and crafts).

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEUPrIP30mOJ4PJyxW40sF9CogL1Kk9x9FUzaJbtsCQo0nMJIBNAeNjs_vJVNTputQQ43avpu7zkMKktoo2njgcg4BGLCzOKxihbiszEOJgibwRAuOmZy9jutCuNc4XbS7CxitoZcSNiPy7cln1QEtB-hQ" width="100%">

On our route we had a catered lunch served at mile 27 of our ride inside the Logan Rogersville Elementary School. Our lunch included sandwiches, fruit, chips, and cookies all from the host Doubletree Hotel. Fortunately for us the lunch stop was almost at the most southern point of our course and we got to enjoy a nice southern tailwind back to Springfield. 

<img src="https://pythiancastle.com/images/pcsw-960.webp" width="100%">

Towards the end of the ride we had scheduled a "ghost tour" of the [Pythian Castle](https://pythiancastle.com/) (offered to all tandem participants as an option) - unfortunately we missed our turn to the Castle and we're too tired to back-track to make the tour.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEXiVqKDI3nCM5Gu6kN7SKdoq8OqoTeU-4IU-_lUuxAJqrAHYyVhgruLfbaip0PmIu_RB6h3aJugOolhDwodlMDn8q_YH06yxl4ZSWZ3Eu_6SdcJKzDZIAyW4k9qeF0EACrFQQ4F_tMKUWGpdONN3A9TEA" width="100%">

Our late Saturday afternoon was spent enjoying the excellent weather outside at [Mother's Brewing Company](https://www.mothersbrewing.com/). Materfamilias - a barrel aged stout was my favorite brew here, followed by the Three Blind Mice (Brown Ale).

Saturday evening's meal was a nice group banquet at the Doubletree Hotel for the entire tandem rally. There were a few small presentations, some door prizes, and an audio-visual show of photos and videos taken during the rally.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AL9nZEX4xwmk4_PbnQ6YtUlQQ6GDlQSM_I3tylORnFzylFgBGWSXLRPcx7r5qbo9_zHOHi1h6XqlaQksvXp8K0oqaYPurlgo5D2GImOdl_fW21YbKtNuyp_DtpvMWK0iOta-MU67StZlmR2zU_FH8frC8dL-RA" width="100%">

**Sunday's Ride - 26 Miles (only distance offered)** A nice out and back ride to the northeast with a rest stop just in Stafford, Missouri. Lots of nice rollers that were perfect for cruising fast on a tandem. If we only could cruise fast enough to keep up with some of teh other tandems :)

Sunday after the ride we met friends for lunch at [Springfield Brewing Company](https://springfieldbrewingco.com/).  After lunch we went over to check-out [Tie and Timber Brewery](https://tieandtimberbeerco.com/) and then came back to Mother's Brewing Company. Finally we finished the evening with pizza at [PaPPo's Pizzeria](https://pappospizzeria.com/) - yet another excellent pizza stop.

We really enjoyed the tandem rally and are definitely planning to return here in 2023. Springfield is an excellent city to visit and offers a wide array of restaurants and breweries... ;)
