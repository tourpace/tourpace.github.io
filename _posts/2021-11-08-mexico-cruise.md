Almost two years since we last were on a cruise ship and we finally felt good enough about the health protocols to head out again. We tried a new ship (for us) the Carnival Dream and a six day itinerary with ports of Costa Maya, Belize City, and Cozumel.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/zRfYik0cbQYhGKR-Y5dY-H5Of7wyukk8oN48WWAHRPNJS8ULDLzz1sOf60gzww10vJkpRN7XCibD6XQMwsWjbZIk0x-IeYBAjFaHVohP6IgdlOMrvj2l5pyTRyg4rw1P45GuR2YGGiM=w2400" width="100%">

**Port stop #1 - Costa Maya, Mexico.** We had been to Costa Maya once before and had visited the [Chacchoben Mayan Ruins](https://www.inah.gob.mx/zonas/105-zona-arqueologica-de-chacchoben). This time we booked our own beach escape at [Almaplena Beach Resort](https://www.almaplenabeachresort.com/). Located about 20 minutes (by taxi) south of the Costa Maya port - it offers a very relaxing all-inclusive beach day. Our package included round-trip transportation from the port, shaded beach loungers, open-bar, lunch, and a very private beach.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/wN-X50rQz1Hw0VChIIFWOvVW0tAuOcILgO2uIanmRWHgWfslU1QpbklgnzSqfkcTCBDcLt97cHv_fYQoNOyVUa1ujTeDTfnIN0ej0QKWY2MYACte4oSZJ-l21cF5Qh_zIuF1zYDJZnA=w2400" width="100%">

We had planned on snorkeling from the beach but the weather was too windy and choppy. So we both relaxed and read - later I took a nice walk down the beach for about two miles. A local dog followed me back from my walk and provided me company.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/tRDlB3s2QVywzfqB2U6WhbdefRuHnTHqdYT4JGzjtVrpbyFQ_5xIUQX_x5ydx5J_ngygbHNuTRb2TxxhAV_oaghmoodBbQ4SuoCPe6ubA6yfno7JqaZUnECRvUu_xyS-KMsAKPJZAqo=w2400" width="100%">

Drinks were very good and the lunch (ceviche, guacamole, chips, and street tacos) was fresh and delicious. Very secluded and private - they limit the total number of guests at 25.



**Port Stop #2 - Belize City, Belize.** In Belize you have to tender to leave the ship - we booked a snorkeling excursion through Carnival that would take us directly toa private island for snorkeling. Unfortunately in was raining hard when we arrived outside of Belize and the excursion was canceled. We just enjoyed a nice relaxing day on the ship.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/2omU33O7id0OLkDXPWJ0DrA49uFFkz_RFtoAz_6l8fEYc6t1mAj1YSVJkS42eSA6kg0Sw5CAKCV7r684yq0OiMTC0aPlL0Rxe-fewzEUxmcIMEgTNQ5NmjA5YgU61g8cKNHsZyPnqz0=w2400" width="100%">

**Port Stop #3 - Cozumel, Mexico.** Cooking school? Three course meal? Margaritas? For something different we put on our chef hats and learned how to prepare ceiviche, Chile rellenos, and dessert tacos at the [Tabasco Chef Maestro Kitchen](https://www.tabascochefmaestro.com/home/).

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/OP0mw5EXfYap-6f2UYiL4oU1asqXmBgEIMv33fKXJfqb4RlXCE7kMMuwohiEydgCr8CMPWcgl6dekNg1cElri4kG5iWtFhVsmfRN70UeRCxu48d7WdVLh6xeiw6nKmWxT0cMvzmU8jc=w2400" width="100%">

The kitchen is divided into individual cooking stations where each couple had everything they need to  prepare a three course meal. The lead chef and support staff were very good and helpful to the culinary challenged (myself included).

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/lrpRkOIWs5gMW9hywTucILbfqJCg3gka8bO79jYh7ZJt_tYYm7hXW0okKAFzzlYvGAZwdSB5nzFpfIjC_yTYWno-ys7Ths8HV1oSVuytQ4wYyH7VliGYpkIvC34FyiOuohoEC9ukqNc=w2400" width="100%">

Our first course to prepare and then plate was ceviche (fresh shrimp and fish). The ceviche is lightly pressed into a round mold to shape it, then placed on fresh lettuce, and topped with pico de gallo.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/NplxlDORR7fVzsM7_a2AARyODYBd_bKyHRDewZhU4n45c1ctJWvq6bWA6hV9sbGLaUkrj9qyGnGc8nVoOQO5Zfogrf_7aaPZBRkKP8id2Xk2PcMd9oZCmax0kBuTjURi9VPYzkKHemc=w2400" width="100%">

After our ceviche was finished - it when into the refrigerator to be served later.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/9OXe_OCeY1q26Rc6FP-jRCo4VJEdDPlHZ2AGkG9U11nDhv6CvHM8cNcOwZ_6duBJJ0CXoT2_Suo1wXYlFQ2ZgbgomiuOufrBFqFvMSVS1GUsu5L-gRHi9j3NNn_EJBoVmQSqu9azrVk=w2400" width="100%">

The second course to prepare was a stuffed chile relleno. The chiles has already been cleaned for us, so we had to just cook the stuffing ingredients, stuff the chile, and plate it all for presentation. The chile rellenos were then placed into a warming oven.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/cXVSVTTSsSQYVT-0aogJ6Jg9uTfA6AYHhWiM5_NNPOQbDMZwp3d8y-uVnGraaOZQWCCH7vf_uiIoHjZjhoAtI_eDKLIRk1Pux-c31hMxgf9Z0Sl8uONAwN0PmyN3DDeepduLF7mxu18=w2400" width="100%">

The final course to prepare were the dessert tacos. We caramelized a variety apples and pears to put into some flour tortillas that we covered in a light cinnamon and sugar.


Open bar was included during the entire class - so the kitchen was very loud and lively. Once we had finished in the kitchen we were taken to an outdoor dining area and seated. Then we got to enjoy our three course meal in a very relaxed setting.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-t5jUlqcspghNxS2gTsbudgFuXd6wbKJq_pk1QQplPyOg1g54Xk1JS4mNPoh2gdtGBrnG3CJUlR13CuTNAYyfFqUERwNksyjPKXW50VBms1seajJ9dW5NkKE5JxavxXU7ObsIZaH7C4=w2400" width="100%">

Cozumel was our last stop on this six day cruise - we had one full at sea day before coming back to Galveston. Even though the rain hampered our one day in Belize, we had a great time getting back out to cruise again.
