**Raton, New Mexico - Often bypassed but worth a visit.** During our numerous travels to Colorado we have always passed by Raton, New Mexico and have never stopped or stayed. So after a week in Cloudcroft we took a short detour north to spend a few days exploring around Raton.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/OffXGFuxvV0K9v26hnX2dsSWAloLPb57wmpfY_XNJXYSfRYYvuwTq2_PYgobCA8psBPs5fsMEoyCWmgvTwTvBYX0U7MWBu928f_pCYFYKaVID8uxxyzz93QwZLekyeYxfHZ4EJTv3OA=w2400" width="100%">

Our lodgings were at another Airbnb - [The Carriage House](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/26947214) - that was walking distance to restaurants and other attractions in downtown Raton. Our plan was to stay three nights so we'd have one day for hiking and a second day to ride bikes.

**Our hiking day included Fisher's Peak State Park, Sugarite Canyon State Park, and Capulin Volcano National Monument.** We started the day with a short 15 mile drive over Raton Pass on Interstate 25 into Colorado to [Fishers Peak State Park](https://www.fisherspeakstatepark.com/). Fishers Peak is a very new park and has one main trail to hike - the Challenge Trail.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/5ZWyGpaKNsNUNUdwsDNhiXBX4DYpbH4S1iZaOLBTUz7wP4gSXx9zLaMUBHCBnB1yfkzvL2c2VWpEOSlXaouegMSUmsnepftL-1fug-mQi7G3zRW-MUCsJAQg3puPntMWfgmaMPEeTDo=w2400" width="100%">

The Challenge Trail often offers a view of Fishers Peak as you climb upward. The trail reminds you of a very steep jeep/four wheeler road that just goes straight up!

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/Uk12WdS9uFnfHR0bIM_JnKWA_Ftg0poVZeMc3GJZGWPzUV3KgrpOjs7MF42G9RbrMrBVlR7T-ZGDWyRZWH_WW3zWVxSuGLbnU7xKpe9bCBJu295PEVmvkH5VfX9jHwd7sxod76dPduU=w2400" width="100%">

According to the park master plan, this trail will eventually continue to the top of Fishers Peak. Currently, the trail ends at one mile up the mountain - but it does offer some nice views.

Looking across the Purgatoire River Valley towards West and East Spanish Peaks.

Our second hike for the day was at **Sugarite Canyon State Park** just northeast of Raton. We chose to hike up the Little Horse Mesa Trail. Another short but very steep hike to the southern edge of Little Horse Mesa.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/PVf6yGrF3eLC84Rv0DnKTud-htIebq3Sg1OQruSHkNiFEGeBma8xMt1yuU6QWo9sm45_UMqKQ4IqLYRJfyl49Hi-BbYGqMFTH35jzvQB8sxZwc39Zt3X8c99UMnMcmG3h5e_Slzevts=w2400" width="100%">

Our final hike destination was the beautiful Capulin Volcano National Monument. We arrived here in the late afternoon and were fortunate that the road to the top of the volcano was still open for the day. There are two trails at the top - the Crater Rim Trail and the Crater Vent Trail. We only had time to hike the one mile Crater Rim Trail. It offers incredible views of the area - you can actually see four (4) states form the top: Colorado, New Mexico, Texas, and Oklahoma.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/fBcmuh3isTibUwEa1bxKQbQCnq-3VKhl4e-KXUjw4vXkIuD234rLZ1HBqh0bZfZqST6nM55KlzTG_20PniVqKzG8VPlhh6JMwgd1okqe6GQWUr118mgbYbMEhkQFEeQBHugFuHKx3N0=w2400" width="100%"> 

**Our second day in Raton was perfect for bike riding to Johnson Mesa.** Our route was from our AirBNB and out New Mexico State Highway 72 to the top of the mesa and back. Just over 2,500 feet of climbing to get up on to the Mesa. Our goal was to ride to the historic Johnson Mesa Church that we had passed by the day before we we drove to Capulin Volcano.

**Just enough restaurants and stores in Raton for a few days.** We had three nights to try the various local restaurants on Downtown Raton. [Colfax Ale Cellar](https://colfaxalecellar.com/) is a small craft brewery that also serves some very good sandwiches. Gate City Craft Bar also offers a good selection of food with beer from Blu Dragonfly Brewery. The local favorite (and ours) for pizza is [Bruno's Pizza](http://www.brunospizzaraton.com/) - one of the few local places open on Sunday evenings. Super Save Discount Food grocery store is the best for stocking up on provisions should you want to cook your own meals.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/RxDKY_V5StYFi5UQeSAjmXXeKzLDqk_Qr5EGqh9GUy7D__0W0Zw8fAGRNCGr3i8GzgecTXLw2H1J7DePJP5PjXG91wu1eh--GSTM50Xa2LmNbucQGwuKPmTWWuOFzGC38t3Szz-0Y_E=w2400" width="100%">

**Raton was definitely worth a stop if you want a good place to anchor as you explore the northeast edge of New Mexico.**
