We had never been to the Florida Keys or the Bahamas - so we really wanted to try a cruise itinerary that would include both - so we booked ourselves on the Carnival Dream from Galveston. Our eight day cruise included stops at Key West, Freeport, Little San Salvador Island, and Nassau.

**Stop #1 - Key West, Florida.** Key West was our first stop and we decided to rent bicycles for the day. A short 1/2 mile walk from the cruise ship pier and we were at [We Cycle](https://wecyclekw.com/) (Old Town) and picked up our rental bikes- Cannondale geared hybrid bikes with locks and front baskets.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/BzaWG3dbzCYaJF2_J3OOOfaF_rh4URpSZEOhiwEX-_ilkYiE3Y1kpN-UKIqbD3qHTrGFtas-P2ec86UGWgS798yHwP2e6CRLAG9pOU8q01Z4mRDmnpcv3saQWmLNnLUMqyEgsTSr4S4=w2400" width="100%">

Our bicycling route was a leisurely loop to include a light-lunch and drink on Stock Island at [Hogfish Bar and Grill](http://www.hogfishbar.com/). Fresh steamed shrimp and a margarita were our rewards at the half-way point on our bike ride. The local bike trails and bike lanes made the journey much easier. We returned back through Key West on the south side of the island. Riding by the throngs of fellow tourists and assorted landmarks. We also rode through [Fort Zachary Taylor Historic State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/fort-zachary-taylor-historic-state-park) to finish our 18-mile ride. 

We returned our bikes and set out for a beer at [The Waterfront Brewery](http://thewaterfrontbrewery.com/). A Key Lime Pie sour beer was a very refreshing treat as we sat along the harbor. Speaking of Key Lime Pie - Heather wanted a slice so she tried one at [Kermit's Key West](http://www.keylimeshop.com/). I wanted ice cream and found the awesome [Gladiator's Ice Cream](https://www.facebook.com/Gladiators-Ice-Cream-Home-Made-519245205125176/), purveyors of all homemade ice cream. The homemade banana ice cream with chunks of fresh banana was one of the best I've ever had.

I think of Key West like a big suburb that was placed on a small island. Throw in some old history, beautiful water with lots of people, and you have an urban oasis?

**Stop #2 - Freeport, Grand Bahama.** In Freeport we booked ourselves with Paradise Cove Beach Resort for a day at their private beach and snorkeling area. They provide round-trip transportation in their bus - the ride is about 25 minutes each way. Our bus ride was full - so be prepared to have very little elbow room. Our driver was very good and shared a good amount of information about Freeport and Grand Bahama.

Upon arriving, they made sure to provide a quick snorkeling orientation. We had reserved two chairs and one umbrella to have a place to keep our stuff. This is not a public beach - so we did not need a locker. The snorkeling area consists of three zones (nearest to farthest from shore) and we explored the first two zones over coral reefs and sea grass. Even though it was shallow enough to stand up, you couldn't as you would harm the coral beds and sea grass.

[![Snorkeling Paradise Cove Beach Resort](http://img.youtube.com/vi/wcLDtXkqIlQ/0.jpg)](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wcLDtXkqIlQ "Snorkeling Paradise Cove Beach Resort")

We were told that sea turtles brazed on these grass beds and eventually we found one!

**Stop #3 - Little San Salvador Island.** Carnival owns this island so they call it "Half Moon Cay" but it is Little San Salvador Island and the beach is originally named "Half Moon Beach". You have to tender to the island as it is not deep enough for a cruise ship to dock. Think of this island as taking your cruise ship off the boat and placing it on a beach. Your sign and sail card works to buy food, drink, and other beach stuff - so cash and credit card are not needed.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/QemLDGvPdlJPx7MCYmJ_NRTAcSy-0OkncFmIL8pfOzV0CN0HOAlMJUIJjGszHH64PmMDQBn1hPnm1ZnUVQIGWPA8LWi_ENqpacCqgiuGzpp4dnbMy9uly5bgxgBQE-SP49ojrGYzKMY=w2400" width="100%">

We snorkeled on our own and there is really only one place to see a reef with fish. Just walk as far away from everybody on the beach heading north and you will come to the end of the beach. No crowds here and you'll see the reef and rocks just out from shore. We snorkeled out to the first set of rocks and reef - any further out and the currents get stronger and can be dangerous.

[![Snorkeling Little San Salvador Island](http://img.youtube.com/vi/tdvDd3oumwo/0.jpg)](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdvDd3oumwo "Snorkeling")

**Stop #4 - Nassau, Bahamas.** We really didn't have much idea of what we wanted to do in Nassau - so we winged it and went for a long walk.

Following the coastline west - we went by Junkanoo Beach,  Fry Town, Saunders Beach, and finally Goodman's Bay Beach. We saw a little bit of everything and mostly local after we left Fry Town. On our way back to the ship we stopped in Fry Town at Twin Brothers for shrimp, conch fritters, and flavored pina coladas - all very tasty and refreshing!

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-G7pOqJriyoAGB82w3oNpKyjrsFnQSkgFh8n-pLPldYdKoN0K_-jYYGDGhzkwpvfgfkiYLR-oQX2XD7OM6ROSWEFjsF8qPQi6zJQ6t6MJWGE2qeegAqHb6inPyEtDw0dD0gut_-n5Ro=w2400" width="100%">

Our final stop before we left was at [Pirate Republic Brewing](http://www.piraterepublicbahamas.com/) for their tasty brews. We were pretty tired when we got back on the ship - calculating we had hiked over nine (9) miles. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/iRSwvka0-OVrbx3lwCPvI8Ma_ruX3Zob4MiST_IwYCM8OMuptkc2KqEUIW2D6rmu1lWg2SsmHPqmEdspm3mukHtGbzOIflaHtdnXRtBFGadZBSQ5Si42VH4YQfVqXr7BnzzOZ0N9G9E=w2400" width="100%">

After Nassau we had two leisurely sea days cruising back to Galveston. We had great weather and a very nice time exploring some new places.
