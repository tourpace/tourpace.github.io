Cruising is almost back to normal and we decided to hop on the Carnival Vista for a week to celebrate our 43rd anniversary. It was an itinerary that we had done twice before with stops in Roatan (Honduras), Belize City (Belize), and Cozumel (Mexico). A last minute discount upgrade moved us to a balcony room on the sixth deck.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/8Ja24OhAgfW95M0t49U1i4xQoEXsz9Cm7BHIVEVX8FaI3NOmUCEGKhCSirA44MR5bRoDFIUhIM7ENbZOxvn7Vmn7jzPTlw5WNwO5x16oCQ1RwDnghzpMOYcXvwv_EixYQxG70-9nNPg=w2400" width="100%">

**Port Stop #1 - Roatan Honduras.** On our previous two trips to Roatan we had spent the one day on Mahogany Beach and had visited [Maya Key](https://www.mayakey.com/) for snorkeling and lunch.

This time we booked a day of snorkeling with [2K's Adventure Tours](http://www.2ksadventurestours.com/) that would give us transportation and snorkeling at Roatan's West Bay area on the far side of the island.

After disembarking at Carnival's port in Mahogany Bay, we had a short walk outside the shops, past the security check-point to meet our tour. Just a short walk up one hill and down another we found the mass of independent tour operators waiting for cruise passengers. 2K's Adventure Tour Staff were easy to find and we were whisked away in a private vehicle with our driver/guide.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/Dkyg_-Yt2ktR55KG91yTNgjZwKczlWTsRm0ETzD7__fAh349NPnY2JmDKLdEx43nPmHulXUocO0AOztSpG8YN0TW08n8c7zvl2QLqsvI8Fyf-KdKHBba2kQgnDWqOqbr1AQS2NpYL8s=w2400" width="100%">

We had several options for possible stops on our way to and from West Beach for snorkeling. We decided for a stop at a scenic overlook in Coxen Hole and [Mayak Chocolates](https://mayakchocolates.negocio.site/) on the way to West Bay.

Our driver/guide took us to the Bananarama Dive and Beach Resort area where we met our boat driver/snorkeling guide. A very short boat ride took us to a nearby reef that had plenty of fish.

**Cruising from Roatan to Belize - Are we sinking???** After a busy day in Roatan we ended up back in our room before 10 p.m. and fell asleep. About 30 minutes later I swear I heard water running - I figured one of us left the bathroom faucet on, so I got up to turn it off. When I stepped out of the bed on to the floor I was standing in about a foot of water. The sound I heard was water sloshing arouns in our room. I opened our cabin door and a small wave and rush of water came into our room from the hall. All I could think was why were we standing in water - were we sinking? No alarm had been sounded so I wasn't sure what was really happening.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/aqiJa4JHcBQc7q7Q-AA4m-rV_7aTu3anjeD0qWuMZ6_O5qM0379Bh4SdQWj-75gHgcemsJG3ZeXmHSXSS_xp9oufzbEprAg4Z_UAAT3PJgK-ce3LMjl1hxwb0nfnvSeoI2JxOi5dHuI=w2400" width="100%">

No, we weren't sinking, an interior room just down the hall (that was empty) had a fresh water pipe overhead that had burst. The water filled that room and then finally made its way out and down the hall. We were evacuated by staff back out to the Promenade Deck. It was after 11 p.m. and I decided to visit Guest Services to see if we could get another room. Guest Services had not yet learned of this mishap but they immediately got us a new Spa Balcony room on deck 14. 

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/PrumncBUBc_XtBF8Sic38Kfv1O0oMpfOTHTl3wRNKOjewMbQtu4wCP1tezTsCH5XTMtTNNRQ348iU2JRT82nGx1Umv4ViIT25nTMzYKm3zpoxi6eqg8r6EknJCKaFeuQtfzjBcNLe1A=w2400" width="100%">

Unfortunately we could not get back to our flooded room since staff was working on the leak and clean-up. We were barefoot and in our sleep clothes. We would end up retrieving our belongings and suitcases the next morning. The only personal items that got wet in our room were our suitcases. We successfully dried out our suitcases and everything was good. Carnival later gave us a $150 credit to our account for the inconvenience and we were enjoying an upgraded room.

**Port Stop #2 - Belize City, Belize.** We canceled a snorkeling excursion in Belize since our flood night was long and we were tired from all the disruption and excitement.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/7tiFGcahEP8UEcWLf_kFbWJ6AUnqRuvhvNAH8gncOgNRfTyvoMfuG5qmVRCPkYDCBGA9Vv8K-3Z7kqJ2DNtX-_oN0IzMojiANID0CCGqcFNX_P3_BmwQfu3iRGtLP7VKgYKK9XAspfY=w2400" width="100%">

**Port Stop #3 - Cozumel, Mexico.** If you cruise from Galveston almost every itinerary will include Cozumel as a port. I think we had been here at least 10 times before and we always find something new to try. This time we booked a local food tour with [Cozumel Food Tours](https://www.cozumelchef.com/services/foodtourscozumel/). We met our guide Gerry at the MEGA Soriana supermarket to start our tour. We were the only ones that had booked the tour for the day so it was just us, Gerry, and the taxi driver that took us around.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/JXCC8bR9m7Wcs7y3bf4WwrLAZt01hlhYcnM964p36J4GTILVT4128uU2w0P6ojDSMvYiBEWQolV12-GAF6f_HlQAT8VFeR-JyenAoyUUdU_1xz6dFp50prvXaic53bXRPiSbkoJx18I=w2400" width="100%">

Our first stop was at [Antojitos Dona Pili](http://www.antojitospili.com/) for quesadillas and flavored  drinks. Our favorite was the [Huitlacoche](https://www.thespruceeats.com/what-is-huitlacoche-or-cuitlacoche-2342705) (corn fungus) quesadilla. We ate it first before they showed us where it comes from - it looks disgusting on corn where it grows, but it was very good!

My favorite drink was the Limon con Chia - limeade with Chia seeds. Again, this drink looked very suspect but it was very tasty and refreshing.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/BGMrASQ7EyVkJgD8CVYVonF88AvKa3mjejrY5kBqYgm8xmLajdCduxVcw7AyvBJzc8hirT1GyezF7LXm27gdCNXEQqVutzfpw_5e-0TSgPClAscuU4MvWI2-VF4Kq3pZapj1cHC5CHA=w2400" width="100%">

Our second food tasting was at Taqueria Maya for their Cochinta Pibil, a traditional Mexican pork dish. Served on corn tortillas with red onions. The pork here is slow cooked overnight with a variety of spices. I tried the Montejo beer for my beverage to compliment the Cochinta. It was all very simple and tasty!

Our next stop was at Mercado Municipal, the largest open food market for food in Cozumel. Here Gerry toured us through the vendor stalls of spices, produce, fruit, chicken, beef, fish, and baked goods.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/lMFBa___2T4jPI73VRfktU8a4tY00XGnJA0c6JZgKXSjsZhWES4s2f-ljvKaUU_BS3QWszwbKYqeBlJpKldqggP1-Xze8n130V5yT9XvdldFdg06c0NU1heLgAJIVUH_oqhr656FcLo=w2400" width="100%">

The third food tasting was at Las Flamitas for Sopa de Lima (Mexican Lime Soup). It was very spicy and had a good lime flavor - but on a warm and humid day, it was not appropriate for me. I'd really need a cold day to appreciate the warm soup and hot spice.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/Pjj6e9hspTSPdbZWI4J3Ke9YLrHN_7bbPqg4NdyCIp4H_26M4LS6vkp4pxLbiXTr5I9mp61G5RsKJwWe52GZhnCAySi-Z45IVaoVsCn1XDmu5W-cDzqzZeB2l4f9eiQg80i8Mf13DRc=w2400" width="100%">

On to Pescadaria San Carlos for our main course of fresh ceviche and fried Red Snapper. At this restaurant you pick out fresh fish from a cooler and then it is all prepared immediately in the adjacent kitchen. It was one of the best Red Snapper dishes I have ever had - very simple but so much flavor.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/FRDtSzZcBLXLMb9F92uyiBG8xy-7SEUzVgFQQgTIueicJvI10FwiKNdMONOQjj4VtDI2XDtMn-Pq4k-lqXse2r0HjWvVwArT7O6oWNqHEUC06upRQsQpIaDiWH7l7Go0a3r8IDMviMk=w2400" width="100%">

Our final stop was at La Conzumelena Bakery for some some dessert pastries. I had some sort of apple filled concoction that was very filling.

<img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/bNhuZcPb13_NOyvPfA_bSL_H-tb2vFwVwau-s0D6DFK3p2vUFAAALiHSCQLW5-5RBvHISL0lP6aE9p-Baas-TwjtrxAJKwF6-qUfCikuJbP2uIHJg_eaHZI2yb1dA6_urgD4YxwtKTg=w2400" width="100%">

We really enjoyed our local food tour in Cozumel and definitely had more than enough to eat. Cozumel Food Tours also offers a cooking class that we plan to do on our next visit to Cozumel. We finished our cruise with a relaxing at sea day before arriving back to Galveston. Overall a very interesting and eventful week in the Western Caribbean.
