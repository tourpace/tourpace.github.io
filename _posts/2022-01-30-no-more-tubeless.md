**Does riding tubed tires make me a curmudgeon?** I ride my gravel bike on pavement and gravel. I prefer wider tires to narrow tires, low tire pressures to higher pressures, and ease of maintenance. So I should be riding tubeless, right???

I have embraced a few of the newer trends like thru-axles and disc brakes... but I still prefer a good steel frame, 8 or 9 speed drive trains, bar-end shifters, and cables for brakes and shifting. I like it when bike parts are easy to repair and fix, especially on the road.

My experiences with tubeless tires have been far from ideal when involving maintenance of any type. Thorns and small wires in the tire were easy to deal with - otherwise I am not a fan.

So, I'm back on good old clincher tires with tubes.

**What I miss about tubeless tires:**
* Less chance of flats from thorns / small wires - For small punctures that the sealant will fill

**What I don't miss about tubeless tires:**
* Large cuts / tears still require a tire boot and a tube
* Large cuts / tears make a mess all over the place with sealant 
* Difficulty of installation - don't get me started, everything has to be just right to get the tire seated
* Pain to maintain when problems happen - requires reinstalling the tire, rim tape,and sealant
* More expensive - tires cost more, plus special rim tape, valve cores, and sealant

I went and rode some of our nearby gravel routes on my tubed tires and I really didn't notice enough difference in ride quality and handling for all the headaches that I had with with tubeless tires. But now if I flat, I can get a tube changed quickly and the tire will seat easily. Also if I had to boot a tire due to a larger cut - I would always prefer the non-tubeless tire, so much easier to mount.

I really like things that work (tubed tires) and I can maintain tubed tires and tubes without the all the tubeless Voodoo. The cost savings of tubed tires is also a big plus for a curmudgeon like me. I had been riding tubed tires for over 40 years and I am no longer convinced of the presumed benefits of tubeless on any bicycle other than a mountain bike.
